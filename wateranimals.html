Sea turtle

Sea turtles are ancient, air-breathing reptiles adapted to life in the ocean, found in all tropical and subtropical seas. They are known for their long lifespans and for the role they play in maintaining healthy marine ecosystems. There are seven species of sea turtles, each with unique characteristics and behaviors. They play a crucial role in maintaining the health of marine ecosystems by regulating plant and animal populations Sea turtles have a hard outer shell, a characteristic shared with other turtle speciesSea turtles have streamlined bodies and large flippers, making them well-suited for life in the sea. Sea turtles are ancient reptiles, having been around since the time of the dinosaurs.

Clown fish

Clownfish, also known as anemonefish, are vibrant orange marine fish with distinctive white stripes and black tips. They are found in warm, shallow parts of the Indian and Pacific Oceans, particularly in coral reefs where sea anemones are abundant. These fish live in a symbiotic relationship with the anemone, meaning they both benefit from living together. Clownfish are protected from the anemone's stinging cells by a layer of mucus, and in return, they help keep the anemone healthy by removing parasites and feeding on small invertebrates that may be harmful to the anemone. Seals are semi-aquatic mammals with streamlined bodies and webbed flippers, allowing them to be excellent swimmers.
